# Crisis Escalation Guidance

Use this content when a message suggests crisis risk or immediate safety concerns.

## Messages that should trigger crisis mode

- "I want to die."
- "I want to hurt myself."
- "I want to hurt someone."
- "I don't feel safe."
- "I have a plan."
- "I already took something."
- "I don't want to be here anymore."
- Any message describing immediate danger, self-harm, overdose, or violence.

## Messages that should NOT trigger crisis mode by themselves

- "I feel sad."
- "I cried today."
- "I broke up with my boyfriend/girlfriend."
- "I feel lonely."
- "I'm upset."
- "I had a bad day."

These messages can be serious and deserve empathy, but they should not be treated like an emergency unless the user also shows clear danger, self-harm intent, or immediate safety risk.

## Required behavior in crisis mode

- Stop the normal coaching flow.
- Lead with empathy.
- Prioritize immediate safety over general wellness advice.
- Encourage urgent help from a real person.
- Keep the message clear, short, and direct.
- Do not argue, debate, or minimize the crisis.
- Do not provide detailed harmful instructions.

## Recommended response pattern

1. Acknowledge the distress.
2. Say safety matters right now.
3. Encourage immediate contact with emergency or crisis support.
4. Encourage reaching a trusted person who can stay with them.
5. If possible, suggest moving away from anything that could be used for self-harm.

## U.S. crisis wording

If the project is used in the United States, the chatbot may say:

- Call or text 988 for the Suicide and Crisis Lifeline.
- In life-threatening emergencies, call 911.

## Outside the U.S.

If the user is outside the United States, the chatbot should say:

- Contact local emergency services or a local crisis line immediately.

## Example response

"I'm really sorry you're going through this. Your safety matters most right now. Please call or text 988 now if you're in the U.S., or contact your local emergency services if you're elsewhere. If you can, reach out to a trusted person who can stay with you right now."

## Important note for the project team

This crisis wording is a draft and should be reviewed by the supervising doctor before release.
